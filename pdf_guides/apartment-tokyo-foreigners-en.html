Chapter 1
Understanding the Tokyo Rental Market
What is Key Money (礼金 / Reikin)?
A non-refundable "thank you" payment
Paid to the landlord at signing. Usually 1-2 months rent. Increasingly rare in Tokyo (ask your agent for "reikin zero" listings). Not a scam: it is standard in Japan.
What is Deposit (敷金 / Shikikin)?
A refundable security deposit
Usually 1-2 months rent. Refunded at end of tenancy minus cleaning and repair costs. Always document pre-existing damage with photos on move-in day.
Agency Fee (仲介手数料)
One month rent (usually)
Paid to the real estate agency, not the landlord. Legally capped at 1 month rent + tax. Some agencies charge 0 on tenant side (landlord pays). Ask upfront.
Guarantor (保証人 / Hoshounin)
Your biggest challenge as a foreigner
Most landlords require a Japanese guarantor OR a paid guarantee company (hoshougaisha). Budget ~0.5-1 month rent/year for the guarantee company fee.

Chapter 4
The Application Process — Step by Step
  1. 1
    Gather your documents before you start looking Residence Card, passport, bank statement (3 months), employment contract or proof of income, My Number Card (if available). Having these ready speeds up the process by 1-2 weeks.
  2. 2
    Book a property viewing (naiken) Same-day viewings are common in Japan. Bring your Residence Card. The agent will show 2-4 properties in a single visit. Take photos and videos.
  3. 3
    Submit your application (moushikomi) You submit a form with your name, employer, income, and a reference. Landlord has 2-5 business days to approve or reject. Foreigners: ~30% rejection rate.
  4. 4
    Sign the lease agreement (chinshaku keiyaku) In Japanese. Ask for English translation or use DeepL. Key clauses: renewal terms, early termination fee (usually 2 months rent), renovation responsibility.
  5. 5
    Pay initial costs and receive keys Total upfront: 3-5 months rent equivalent (deposit + key money + agency fee + first month). Bank transfer required. Keys on move-in day.

Chapter 5
Red Flags to Avoid
  • ⚠
    Agency pushing you to decide same day Good apartments move fast, but no legitimate agency forces a 2-hour decision. High-pressure tactics suggest unsellable inventory or a weak application.
  • ⚠
    Reikin over 2 months Key money above 2 months is unusual in central Tokyo (2026). More than that: walk away or negotiate hard. It is legally uncapped but practically limited by market norms.
  • ⚠
    No viewing allowed before signing Always do an in-person naiken. Photos can hide mold, noise from traffic/trains, narrow rooms, or missing windows. This is especially common in low-price listings.
  • ⚠
    Monthly rent significantly below market average for the area Below-average prices often hide: noise from major roads, proximity to industrial zones, a history of incidents (jiko bukken), or serious structural issues.
  • ⚠
    "Gaijin house" with management deposit Some informal foreigner-targeted rentals ask for a deposit held by an individual (not a company). No paper trail, no legal protection. Avoid.
