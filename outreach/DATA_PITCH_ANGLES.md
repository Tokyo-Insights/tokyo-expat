# Data pitch angles -- boucle "data -> backlinks presse"

But: transformer notre data en backlinks. Les journalistes/blogueurs ADORENT la data
originale et chiffree. On pitche un angle + le lien /data (ou un chart), ils citent avec
attribution + lien -> autorite de domaine -> nos articles rankent mieux. Boucle.
Se branche sur la machine guest-post existante (backlink_draft_stocker / followup_watcher).
Page presse citable: tokyo-expat.com/en/press. Voir [[project_virtuous_loops]].

## Les 5 angles data (du plus fort au plus niche)
1. **"Tokyo is one of the cheapest major cities to rent in the world right now"**
   - Hook: studio central < 800 US$/mois vs NYC 3000+, Londres, Sydney, HK. Yen faible.
   - Chart: tokyo-rent-by-station.png. Article: /en/blog/tokyo-rent-vs-world-cities
   - Cibles: finance/cost-of-living writers, expat-finance blogs, "digital nomad" sites.
2. **"Tokyo condo prices rose ~30% in 4 years, and the centre pulled away"**
   - Hook: +30% ville, Chuo +60% / Minato +52% vs peripherie +16%. Chart: tokyo-price-map.png / dumbbell.
   - Cibles: real-estate/investment media, Japan-business newsletters.
3. **"The real cost of living in Tokyo, station by station"**
   - Hook: 50 stations classees, prime du centre. Chart: tokyo-rent-by-station.png.
   - Cibles: relocation sites, travel/lifestyle Japan blogs, university international-student pages.
4. **"Furnished, no-guarantor apartments: how foreigners actually rent in Tokyo"**
   - Hook: contourner garant + 4-6 mois d'avance. Article: /en/blog/furnished-apartments-tokyo-guide
   - Cibles: moving-to-Japan guides, expat forums/blogs, HR-relocation resources.
5. **"What a family flat really costs vs a studio in Tokyo"** (x2-2.8)
   - Chart: tokyo-layout-gap.png. Cibles: family-expat blogs, international-school communities.
