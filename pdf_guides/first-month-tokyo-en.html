Your First Month in Tokyo — 2026
Week
1
Legal Foundation: Residence Card + SIM
These two items unlock everything else. Do them on Day 1.
🏛️
Day 1 — Priority
Register at Ward Office (Shiyakusho)
Bring: passport, visa, accommodation address. You receive your Residence Card (在留カード). Without it: no bank, no lease, no NHI.
📱
Day 1-2
Get a Japanese SIM card
Sakura Mobile (airport pickup available) or IIJmio. Requires passport + Residence Card. 3GB plan: ~1,200 JPY/month.
🚃
Day 1
Get a Suica / Pasmo IC card
At any JR station. Load 3,000-5,000 JPY. Works on all trains, buses, taxis, convenience stores, vending machines.
💴
Week 1
Withdraw cash at 7-Eleven ATM
7-Eleven ATMs accept all foreign cards (Visa, Mastercard). Japan is still largely cash-based. Keep 50,000 JPY on hand at all times.
Ward Office Hours
Mon-Fri 8:30-17:00. Some wards open 1st/3rd Saturday.
Address format in Japan
Block/lot number system. Get it exact from landlord or booking.
Sakura Mobile SIM
Data SIM from 1,100 JPY/mo. Voice plan: 2,200 JPY/mo. No guarantor needed.
Airport SIM counter
Terminal 1+2 at Narita, T2+3 at Haneda. Open 7:00-23:00.
Week
2
Banking: Open Your Japanese Account
Required for salary, rent payments, and tax. Most banks require 6+ months of residence. These do not.
🏦
Best option
PayPay Bank (online, English)
100% online setup. No waiting period. English UI. Free account. Fastest option for new arrivals. Requires My Number within 6 months.
🏦
Alternative
Sony Bank
Excellent for international transfers. English app. Low ATM fees. Multi-currency account available. Apply online in 10 minutes.
💱
Tip
Keep using Wise for transfers
Wise (formerly TransferWise) has the best EUR/USD to JPY rates. Use it alongside your Japanese account for international transfers.
💳
Week 2-3
Set up PayPay digital wallet
Japan's most popular cashless payment. Link to Japanese bank account. Accepted at 90%+ of convenience stores, restaurants, taxis.

Week
3
Social Protection: Health Insurance + Pension
Both are mandatory by law for all residents in Japan. Do not skip this.
🏥
Mandatory
National Health Insurance (NHI)
Apply at ward office. Cost: 5,000-25,000 JPY/month based on income. Covers 70% of medical costs. Bring: Residence Card + passport.
🪪
Mandatory
National Pension (Kokumin Nenkin)
~16,980 JPY/month (2026). If low income, apply for full or partial exemption (menjo). Bring income documents to ward office.
🩺
After NHI
Register with a local clinic
Find a "kakariitsuke-i" (family doctor) near your home. Most accept walk-ins. Google "clinic near me + your ward" — many have English staff.
🛡️
Bridge insurance
Keep SafetyWing until NHI activates
NHI takes 1-2 weeks to activate. Keep your travel/expat insurance active during this gap. SafetyWing Nomad Insurance: ~$56/month.

Week
4
Utilities + My Number Card Application
Set up electricity, gas, internet, and apply for your official Japanese ID card.
⚡
Day 1 in apartment
Electricity (TEPCO)
Online signup at tepco.co.jp. English available. Activate same day. Flip the breaker in your apartment fuse box first.
🔥
Book early
Gas (Tokyo Gas)
Technician visit required. Book at gas.or.jp. 1-2 week wait is common. You must be present. Take a half-day off work.
🌐
Book immediately
Internet (NURO Fiber / SoftBank)
Installation: 2-4 week wait. Use mobile data in the meantime. NURO: 2Gb fiber, ~4,950 JPY/month. Softbank Air: no construction, ~5,368 JPY.
🪪
Apply week 3-4
My Number Card (Kojin Bango Card)
Apply at ward office or online (mynaportal.digital.go.jp). Collection takes 3-6 weeks. Required for taxes, some banking, and government services.
