Japan Relocation Checklist 2026
📅
Phase 1
6 to 3 Months Before Arrival
  • Determine your visa typeWorking Holiday, HSP, Spouse, Student, or Business Manager
  • Start visa applicationGather documents: bank statements, employment letter, CV
  • Research neighborhoodsShinjuku, Shibuya, Setagaya, Koto, Adachi by budget/lifestyle
  • Set your housing budgetTokyo average: 80,000-130,000 JPY/month for 1K/1LDK
  • Book temporary accommodationMonthly apartment or guesthouse for first 1-2 months
  • Notify your home country bankInternational transfers, notify of address change
  • Open a Wise accountBest rates for JPY transfers, essential for expats
  • Sort out health insuranceSafetyWing for transit period before National Health Insurance
  • Decide what to ship vs. sellJapan has strict customs rules. Electronics/furniture often not worth shipping
  • Get International Driving PermitValid for 1 year in Japan. Required if you plan to drive
✈️
Phase 2
1 to 2 Months Before Arrival
  • Book your flightsOne-way or round-trip depending on visa requirements
  • Arrange document apostillesBirth certificate, marriage certificate if applicable
  • Research Japanese language basicsHiragana, katakana, key phrases for daily life
  • Set up mail forwardingNotify all important services of your future absence
  • Cancel or transfer subscriptionsGym, streaming services, insurance policies
  • Scan all important documentsPassport, diploma, birth certificate, store in cloud
  • Research SIM card optionsSakura Mobile, IIJmio, LINEMO — buy at airport or online
  • Download essential appsGoogle Maps, Google Translate, Hyperdia, Suica, LINE
Pro tip — Carry-on essentials Pack your passport, all visa documents, temporary accommodation address, and 100,000 JPY cash in your carry-on. Japanese customs may ask for your address in Japan.

Phase 3
First 48 Hours in Japan
  • Register your address at the Ward Office (Shiyakusho)Bring passport + visa. You receive your Residence Card (Zairyu Card). Mandatory within 14 days but do it immediately.
  • Get a SIM cardSakura Mobile and IIJmio are foreigner-friendly. Airport counters available at Narita and Haneda.
  • Get a Suica card (IC card)At any JR station or 7-Eleven ATM. Load 3,000-5,000 JPY. Works on trains, buses, convenience stores.
  • Locate nearest convenience store, supermarket, and pharmacy7-Eleven and FamilyMart ATMs accept foreign cards. Matsumoto Kiyoshi for pharmacy.
  • Check in to temporary accommodationConfirm check-in time in advance. Japanese guesthouses often have strict rules.
Important: Residence Card comes first Without your Residence Card address, you cannot open a bank account, sign a lease, or register for National Health Insurance. Do this on Day 1.
🏦
Phase 4
First 2 Weeks
  • Open a Japanese bank accountPayPay Bank or Sony Bank: 100% online, English UI, foreigner-friendly
  • Start apartment searchUse foreigner-friendly, bilingual agencies. Many of the best options never reach the big listing sites.
  • Apply for National Health InsuranceAt your ward office. Required for all residents. ~5,000-20,000 JPY/month
  • Apply for National Pension (Kokumin Nenkin)At ward office. Low income: apply for exemption (menjo)
  • Set up PayPay or LINE PayCashless payment: essential for taxis, convenience stores, restaurants
  • Explore your neighborhood on footFind the closest station, post office, ward office, hospital

Phase 5
First Month: Settle In
  • Sign your apartment leaseBudget: 2-3 months deposit + agency fee. Key money (reikin) in some areas.
  • Set up electricityTEPCO in Tokyo: online signup, English available. Done in 1 day.
  • Set up gasTechnician visit required. Tokyo Gas. Must be present (half-day).
  • Set up internetNURO Fiber or SoftBank Air. 2-4 week install delay is common.
  • Register new address at ward officeUpdate your Residence Card address within 14 days of moving in.
  • Apply for My Number CardAt ward office. Takes 3-6 weeks. Required for taxes, some banking.
  • Get a personal hanko (seal)Optional but useful. Convenience stores have machines (Shachihata).
  • Visit a local clinic for health checkRegister with a family doctor (kakaritsuke-i) near your home.
📋
Phase 6
First 3 Months: Administration
  • Collect My Number CardBring notification letter + ID to ward office.
  • Convert driving license (if applicable)At JAF center. Bring JP license translation + original. 3-6 months wait.
  • Register for e-Tax (online tax portal)Required for annual tax return in March. nta.go.jp portal.
  • Update bank account with My NumberRequired by law. Do it at your branch or app.
🔄
Phase 7
Ongoing: Stay Compliant
  • File annual tax return (kakutei shinkoku)Due by March 15. Required if income exceeds 2M JPY/year or multiple sources.
  • Renew Residence Card before expiryCheck expiry date. Apply 3 months before at Immigration Services Agency.
  • Update address after any moveWithin 14 days at ward office. Also update bank, NHI, pension.
  • Review your visa renewal timelineMany visas: 1-3 year terms. Plan renewals 6 months in advance.
